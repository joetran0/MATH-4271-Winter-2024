Overview of MATH 4271 Dynamical Systems

Description

Iterations of maps and differential equations; phase portraits, flows; fixed points, periodic solutions and homoclinic orbits; stability, attraction, repulsion; Poincar√© maps, transition to chaos. Applications: logistic maps, interacting populations, reaction kinetics, forced Van der Pol, damped Duffing and Lorenz equations.

Prerequisites

SC/MATH 2270 3.00; SC/MATH 1021 3.00 or SC/MATH 2221 3.00 or SC/MATH 1025 3.00.

Textbook/Resources

  • Course Textbook: Perko L., Differential Equations and Dynamical Systems, Vol. 7, (2013 Edition)Click to view (Additional Reference)

Covered Material

  1. Solving Linear Systems of ODEs
  2. Introduction to Dynamical Systems
    • Dynamical flows
    • Qualitative analysis of scalar autonomus dynamical systems (1D flows)
    • Some definitions
  3. Phase Plane Analysis of Linear Systems
    • Classification of equilibrium
    • Local stability analysis and phase plane
    • Extension to 3D linear systems
  4. Dynamical theory of linear and nonlinear autonomous ODEs: existence and uniqueness
  5. Nonlinear systems and local/global stability
  6. Theory of Bifurcation
  7. Oscillations in Nonlinear Systems
  8. Applications of ecology and epidemiology
  9. Discrete systems and chaos

Real Prerequisites

Familiarity with concepts from Linear Algebra, i.e. linear independence, determinants, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and concepts from Differential Equations, i.e. solving first order ODEs, solving homogeneous equations, etc.
